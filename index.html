Introduction

Purpose

This style guide provides essential standards and practices for creating consistent training and curriculum materials.

Our material adheres to the guidelines outlined in Pega Technical Documentation Guidebook and Pega Brand Guidelines unless otherwise stated.

Naming convention

Structure and format

Zero padding: Always use two digits for each level of the hierarchy (01, 02, ..., 10).

Hierarchical naming: Each subfolder inherits and extends the parent folder's number (0101 is a subfolder of 01).

No punctuation: Do not use dots, underscores, or hyphens in folder names.

Scalable: This format allows for unlimited expansion without renaming existing folders.

Examples

01 Parent folder 1
    0101 Child folder 1
        010101 Nested folder 1
        010102 Nested folder 2

02 Parent folder 2
    0201 Child folder 1
        020101 Nested folder 1
        020102 Nested folder 2

VISUALS

Whitespace

  • Whitespace is the empty space between elements that enhances readability and balances layout.
  • Proper use of whitespace creates a clean and organized design.
  • Insufficient whitespace leads to cluttered content, making it harder to read and process.

Alignment

Alignment is arranging elements along a common edge or center to create visual order and enhance readability.

Not Recommended: Misaligned elements result in a disorganized, hard-to-follow design.

Recommended: Consistent alignment of text and images creates an organized, easy-to-read layout.

Visual hierarchy

Visual hierarchy organizes design elements to guide viewer attention in a clear, prioritized order.

Not Recommended: Disrupted element order breaks the natural reading flow, causing confusion.

Recommended: Elements follow a logical flow, directing focus naturally from text to image.

Image quality

Images should be sharp and clear to ensure all elements are easily distinguishable.

Not Recommended: Blurry visuals reduce clarity, making details hard to read and understand.

Recommended: Clear visuals enhance user understanding by making details easy to read.

Image scale

Images should fit the design without losing clarity or proportion.

Not Recommended: Stretched or compressed images cause distortion, reducing professionalism.

Recommended: Properly scaled images maintain clarity and proportion, avoiding distortion.

Writing

Learner-centric approach

International audience: Avoid slang and regional references. Maintain a professional tone.

Inclusive language: Use respectful, unbiased, and inclusive language for all learners.

Plain language: Be specific. Avoid jargon; if a technical term must be used, define it in context.

Acronyms and abbreviations

Spell out acronyms on the first reference, followed by the shortened version in parenthesis.

Not recommended: (ILT) instructor-led training.
Recommended: instructor-led training (ILT).

When expressing plural acronyms and abbreviations, use a lowercase "s" without an apostrophe.

Not recommended: MLP's
Recommended: MLPs

Use your best judgment to determine if the acronym or abbreviation is common enough to warrant not defining it.

Not recommended: wireless fidelity
Recommended: Wi-Fi

Ampersand

Do not use an ampersand (&), unless it is part of a proper name.

Not recommended: Update the Stages & Steps.
Recommended: Update the Stages and Steps.

Capitalization

In general use sentence case; except for proper nouns, Pega-specific terms, and mission titles.

Not recommended: This is Headline Case
Recommended: This is sentence case.

Avoid all capital lettering when referring to UI elements.

Not recommended: BUTTON
Recommended: Button

Capitalize the names of rule forms when the sentence is referring to the actual rule form.

Not recommended: the property form
Recommended: the Property form

Commas

Use an Oxford comma for a series of three or more items.

Not recommended: You can import, rename or reclassify a Case Type.
Recommended: You can import, rename, or reclassify a Case Type.

Use a comma after introductory words, clauses, and phrases.

Not recommended: For more information see the release notes.
Recommended: For more information, see the release notes.

Date and Time

Use the 12-hour format, followed by a capitalized AM or PM with a space between.

Not recommended: 2:15am
Recommended: 2:15 AM

Write full month names and use four-digit years to avoid date confusion. If a date must be expressed numerically, use the format YYYY-MM-DD.

Not recommended: 3/03/2020 or 04/05/09
Recommended: March 3, 2020 or 2009-04-05

Hyphens and Slashes

Use a hyphen for compound words and negative numbers.

Not recommended: Interface specific
Recommended: Interface-specific

Avoid "either/or" and "and/or;" use full phrases instead.

Not recommended: You can choose to use either the AWS/Apache server, or both.
Recommended: You can choose to use either the AWS or the Apache server, or both.

Latin phrases

Avoid Latin terms or abbreviations.

Not recommended: etc., e.g.
Recommended: and so on, for example, such as

Numbers

Spell out numbers from zero through nine; use numerals for numbers 10 and greater.

Not recommended: 3, forty
Recommended: three, 40

Spell out ordinal numbers in text.

Not recommended: 1st, 3rd, 12th
Recommended: first, third, twelfth

Trademarking

Use ™ only the first time a Pega product name appears in body text. Don’t use ™ in titles, headings, or links.

Voice and tone

Use active voice, meaning the subject of the sentence acts.

Not recommended: The course outline can be accessed by students on the portal.
Recommended: Students access the course outline on the portal.

Write in the present tense and use the second person singular to address the user.

Not recommended: The user clicks Start to initiate the process.
Recommended: Click Start to initiate the process.

AI written content

Reviewing AI written content

AI tools can accelerate content development, but they must be used thoughtfully. This section outlines common issues to watch for when reviewing AI-generated text and provides best practices to ensure quality, accuracy, and consistency with Pega and team standards.

"Remember, AI is a tool, not a crutch. Always review and revise before publishing."
  • Inconsistent application of standards: AI output often lacks alignment with Pega, Pega Academy, and CD team writing conventions, including tone, terminology, and formatting.
  • Unoriginal and formulaic language: AI tends to generate generic phrasing that fails to engage learners or convey instructional intent effectively.
  • Contextual and factual errors: AI may misinterpret technical concepts, omit nuance, or introduce cultural inaccuracies. This can lead to confusion or misinformation.

Ensure consistency in terminology and structure

Maintain consistent noun usage, voice, and parallel sentence structure.

Not recommended: The application leverages a robust framework. This program utilizes multiple libraries.
Recommended: The application leverages a robust framework. This application uses multiple libraries.

Use active voice

Favor active constructions to improve clarity and readability.

Not recommended: The report was written by the team.
Recommended: The team wrote the report.

Limit pronoun overuse

Replace vague pronouns with clear, specific nouns.

Not recommended: This means that...
Recommended: This outcome means....

Simplify technical language

Prefer accessible verbs like "use," "apply," and "implement." Provide definitions for complex terms.

Not Recommended: Utilize the asynchronous operations.
Recommended: Use asynchronous operations.

Vary transition phrases

Avoid repetitive sentence starters to maintain reader engagement.

Not recommended: Furthermore, we found that...
Recommended: We also found that...

Choose precise adjectives

Replace vague or exaggerated descriptors with accurate, meaningful alternatives.

Not recommended: It was an extremely tough challenge.
Recommended: It was a difficult challenge.

PowerPoints

Presentation design

Design and layout

Minimal: Keep slides uncluttered; avoid excessive text and overly complex graphics.

Alignment: Align text and graphics consistently across all slides to maintain an organized appearance.

Whitespace: Use ample whitespace to reduce visual fatigue; ensure balance between filled and empty spaces.

Visuals and graphics

Relevance and quality: Use high-quality visuals that directly support or illustrate the content and are relevant to the topic.

Consistency in style: Using flat or isometric styles, keep the visual style consistent throughout the presentation, including icons and diagrams.

Simplification: Convert complex data or processes into simplified infographics or diagrams.
